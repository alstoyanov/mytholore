Hathor: Temple of Joy

Story

Step into the sanctuary of Hathor, where music soothes, love reigns, and laughter lights the heavens. “Temple of Joy” is a luminous and melodic metal ballad to the Egyptian goddess of fertility, celebration, and sacred femininity — the joy-bringer of gods and mortals alike.

Who is Hathor?

Hathor is one of ancient Egypt’s most beloved deities — goddess of love, beauty, dance, music, fertility, and the celestial vault. She was known as the "Mistress of the West" who welcomed the dead into the afterlife and the divine mother of pharaohs.

Depicted with cow horns and the solar disk, or as a beautiful woman playing a sistrum, she was worshipped as a source of joy, ecstasy, and emotional healing. Her temples were places of music, offerings, and life-affirming rituals.
