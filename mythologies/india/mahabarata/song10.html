Karna: The Tragic Son

Story

One of the greatest yet most tragic figures of the Mahabharata, Karna was a warrior like no other, yet cursed from birth. This song tells the heart-wrenching tale of the lost son of Kunti, who was:

⚔️ A Warrior Without a Name:

  • Born of the Sun God, yet abandoned at birth.
  • Raised by charioteers, denied the right to be a Kshatriya (warrior caste).
  • Humiliated by Draupadi, insulted by the world—yet never bowed.

⚔️ A Brother Without Knowing:

  • Unaware that his true brothers were the Pandavas, the very ones he was destined to fight.
  • Sworn to Duryodhana, the man who gave him a throne and friendship when no one else would.
  • Trapped by fate—honor or truth, blood or loyalty?

⚔️ A Warrior Without Mercy:

  • Cursed by Parashurama—his divine knowledge would fail him when he needed it most.
  • Stripped of his divine armor by Indra, leaving him defenseless.
  • His chariot wheel sunk into the earth—the gods themselves conspired against him.
  • Struck down by Arjuna, as Krishna watched in silence.
