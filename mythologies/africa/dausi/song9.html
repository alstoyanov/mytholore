The Fall of Lagadu

Story

The fall of Lagadu in the Dausi is not caused by armies or invasion — it is a collapse from within, a slow spiritual decay foretold by prophecy and accelerated by ambition, sacrifice, and the gods’ silence.

After Bida's betrayal of blood in his pursuit of immortality, the city begins to lose its soul. The sacred drums no longer speak. The priests’ chants go unanswered. The people begin to leave, and those who remain are haunted by echoes of what Lagadu once was.

Eventually, Bida himself is expelled, no longer king, no longer father, no longer heir. He walks the ruins alone, still breathing, yet entirely dead to the kingdom he once sought to rule. His immortality becomes a curse — a sentence to remember every step of Lagadu’s fall.

The Dagomba griots tell this as a cautionary myth: that kingdoms do not fall by swords alone — they fall when leaders forget the cost of blood, and when glory is chased harder than grace.
