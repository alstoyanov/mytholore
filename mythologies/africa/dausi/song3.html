The Kingdom of Lagadu

Story

According to the Dausi epic, the city of Lagadu was more than a capital — it was a spiritual anchor, a kingdom born from the divine vision of Naa Gbewaa, destined to become the heart of Dagbon.

Lagadu was not raised by chance. The ancestors guided its placement, the gods blessed its walls, and ritual shaped its stones. Every pillar and courtyard echoed with the presence of ancestral spirits, while the people came from distant lands, drawn by visions, omens, and the prophetic voice of Gbewaa.

The city symbolized not just order and power, but a sacred covenant — between the living and the dead, between earth and sky. It was said that the very ground beneath Lagadu remembered the footsteps of the gods. Gbewaa's crown did not simply rest on his brow; it radiated through the city itself.

To this day, Lagadu is remembered as the soul of kingship, where the first breath of the nation was taken, and where its heartbeat was etched into time.
