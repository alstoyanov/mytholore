Bida’s Sons and the Price of Immortality

Story

In the Dausi, the story of Bida’s immortality is not one of glory — but of irreversible sacrifice. When prophecies foretold the end of his reign, Bida did not seek a solution through wisdom or peace. Instead, he turned to ritual and blood.

To avoid death, he made the unthinkable choice: he offered his own sons in sacred sacrifice, believing the gods would grant him life unending. And they did — but not without cost. He lived, ageless and untouched by time, but forever marked by the void where his bloodline once stood.

This tale is a warning to all rulers: that immortality can be worse than death if it’s bought by the bones of one’s own. The palace of Lagadu, though still strong in stone, became hollow in soul — haunted not by spirits, but by silence, where the laughter of sons once dwelled.

To the Dagomba, this moment is remembered not as triumph, but as the fracture of a father’s heart — and the beginning of a kingdom’s fall.
