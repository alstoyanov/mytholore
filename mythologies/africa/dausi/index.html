The Dausi

The Dausi resurrects one of Africa’s greatest epic traditions: the tale of the Wagadu, the legendary kingdom of the Soninke people, whose fate is told through cycles of rise, betrayal, and renewal. Rooted in the oral tradition of the griots, the Dausi is both history and myth, a story where gods, ancestors, and mortals are bound together in the eternal struggle for survival and legacy. This album transforms that epic into a torrent of sound—guitars that ring like iron spears, drums that thunder like cavalry across the Sahel, and vocals that echo with the chants of storytellers beneath the desert stars.

Each track is woven from the tapestry of the epic: The Splendor of Wagadu opens with triumphant strength, painting the kingdom’s golden age. The Betrayal of Kaya Magan tears into chaos and bloodshed, capturing the internal divisions that fracture the realm. The Four Disappearances builds with hypnotic rhythms, reflecting the kingdom’s mythic cycles of downfall and rebirth. And The Ancestors’ Call closes the saga with solemn grandeur, invoking the voices of those who kept the story alive through centuries of memory and song.

The Dausi is more than a metal album—it is an homage to Africa’s oldest epics, a bridge between ancient griot poetry and modern sonic force. Through distortion and ritual power, it preserves the story of a people whose myth is as vast as the desert and as enduring as the oral tradition itself. This is myth reborn in fire and rhythm, a storm of memory and prophecy carried forward by the music of eternity.
