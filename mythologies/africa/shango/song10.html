Crown of Lightning

Story

In the pantheon of Yoruba Orishas, Shango is not only fire and fury — he is truth made divine. He is the one who punishes those who lie, break oaths, or deceive for power. He is also the protector of the honest, the giver of strength to the oppressed.

When the drums summon him for judgment, he does not come blindly. He weighs the truth. And when he strikes, it is not rage — it is divine law rendered in thunder.

In the Caribbean, Brazil, and West Africa, he is invoked during trials, justice rituals, and personal conflict. Shango’s lightning is feared not for its violence — but for its clarity. It is the fire that reveals what was hidden.
