The Siege of White castle

Story

The White Castle, a symbol of resistance against Persian rule, stands tall and fortified in the midst of a great war. Its rulers, a tribe of enemies, refuse to yield to Persia’s dominance. When Rostam is tasked with leading the siege, he does not only bring his strength but his strategic mind. The battle is intense, with every inch of the castle defended with ferocity. Rostam, however, is unstoppable. He breaks down the gates, fights valiantly through the enemy lines, and ultimately secures the victory for Persia.

The siege is not just a physical battle; it is a test of Rostam’s courage, leadership, and resilience in the face of seemingly insurmountable odds. It shows the cost of war and the price of glory, as Rostam’s victory comes with sacrifices on both sides. The song captures not only the heroic clash but also the emotional toll of war, highlighting Rostam’s inner conflict as he emerges victorious but haunted by the cost of his triumph.
