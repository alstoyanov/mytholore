Artemis: Moon and Arrow

Story

"Moon and Arrow" is a fierce, untamed anthem sung from the perspective of Artemis, protector of the wild and punisher of those who violate her sacred law. Through tales like Actaeon’s transformation and Niobe’s downfall, Artemis reveals her sharp justice and divine independence. The song embraces her role as a guardian of purity, a hunter, and a goddess who answers only to herself and the moon.

Artemis is the twin sister of Apollo and goddess of the hunt, wilderness, childbirth, and the moon. She is a virgin goddess known for her fierce independence and affinity with wild animals. Often depicted with a silver bow, Artemis punishes arrogance and protects the vulnerable. She is worshiped in wild places and revered by women and hunters alike.
