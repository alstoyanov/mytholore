Story

The Second Labor of Heracles (Hercules in Roman tradition) was the slaying of the Lernaean Hydra, a monstrous, serpent-like creature that dwelled in the swamps near the city of Lerna in the Argolid.

The Hydra was no ordinary beast:

  • It had multiple heads (accounts vary, usually nine), and whenever one was cut off, two more would grow in its place.

  • Its breath and blood were poisonous, deadly to humans and animals alike.

  • A giant immortal head at the center made the Hydra seemingly impossible to kill.

Hercules set out to destroy it as commanded by King Eurystheus. He brought along his nephew Iolaus, who became crucial to the task. As Hercules chopped off each head, Iolaus cauterized the wounds with a burning brand (or torch), preventing new heads from sprouting. Finally, Hercules severed the immortal head and buried it under a heavy rock, ensuring it could never rise again.
