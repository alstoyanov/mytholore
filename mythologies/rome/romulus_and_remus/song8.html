Remus’ Rebellion

Story

Remus speaks for the last time. This track is his emotional defiance—an anthem of rebellion not against Rome, but against what it cost. He leapt over the wall Romulus built, not to mock it, but to question the foundation of power, pride, and prophecy.

This is the voice of a brother who loved too deeply to submit. And it is his final cry before the blade falls.

In Roman lore, Remus jumped over Romulus’ wall, mocking the divine claim his brother made. Romulus killed him in response. Some versions say it was rage; others say it was ritual. But all agree—Rome began with fratricide. This track captures that moment of resistance, heartbreak, and legacy denied.
