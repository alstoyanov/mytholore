She-Wolf’s Call

Story

This track gives a voice to Lupa, the she-wolf who found and nursed Romulus and Remus in the cave of the Palatine Hill. A divine or symbolic creature in Roman myth, she is both beast and goddess—nurturer and predator. This song is her hymn: a fierce, tribal anthem of protection and wild maternal strength.

After being cast into the Tiber River, the infant twins were discovered by a she-wolf. She took them into her den and fed them with her milk. In Roman myth, the she-wolf became an eternal symbol of Rome—feral, strong, and sacred. The Lupa Capitolina statue still stands as a testament to her legacy.

She is more than an animal. She is the Earth Mother, the wild protector, and the first force to recognize the twins’ destiny. In this track, her voice is ancient and divine. The tribal percussion mirrors the heartbeat of the land itself. She does not speak like a human—she howls with truth.
