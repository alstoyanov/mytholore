Songs of Aeneas

Songs of Aeneas gives new voice to one of Rome’s greatest legends—the epic journey of Aeneas, the Trojan hero fated to lay the foundations of an empire. From the smoking ruins of Troy to the destined shores of Latium, this album channels the Aeneid’s fire and sorrow into a storm of sound. The riffs march like legions across burning battlefields, the drums crash like storm-tossed waves, and the vocals echo with the weight of prophecy and sacrifice. This is not just music—it is an odyssey of fate carved in distortion and steel.

Every track unfolds a chapter of the hero’s tale: The Fall of Ilium erupts with chaotic fury, capturing the night Troy was consumed by fire. Flight Across the Sea surges with relentless momentum, waves of rhythm crashing like Neptune’s wrath. The Queen of Carthage weaves haunting melodies and crushing crescendos, embodying the doomed love of Aeneas and Dido. The Descent into the Underworld drops into dark, brooding heaviness, a passage through shadow where Aeneas confronts the souls of heroes and the will of fate. The saga climaxes with The Battle for Latium, a furious storm of riffs and war-cries, before resolving in Foundations of Rome, an anthem that closes the album with both triumph and solemnity.

Songs of Aeneas is a metal epic that bridges myth and destiny. It captures the fire of Troy, the sorrow of exile, the weight of prophecy, and the blood-soaked birth of empire. Through sheer heaviness and mythic grandeur, the record resurrects Aeneas not just as a figure of legend, but as a wanderer, warrior, and father of Rome—his voice echoing through the centuries, now reborn in thunder.
